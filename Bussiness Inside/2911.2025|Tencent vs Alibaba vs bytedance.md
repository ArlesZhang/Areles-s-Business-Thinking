# Tencent vs Alibaba vs Douyin/bytedance:

## A Comparative Table of Revenue Sources Across Different Business Models

This is structured across six business-chain stages:

1. User acquisition
2. Content or product supply
3. Distribution and matching
4. Transaction and fulfillment
5. Payment and financial services
6. Advertising and marketing

Each table explains:
• Whether the company participates in that stage
• How it charges
• Why that stage matters

---

# 1. Tencent (Social Graph + Payment + “Infrastructure Tax” Model)

Tencent does not do heavy logistics or commerce.
Its model is: **build social infrastructure → let others do business → take a tax on traffic and payment**.

| Stage                    | Participation                    | Revenue Mechanism                     | Notes                                              |
| ------------------------ | -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| User acquisition         | Yes (WeChat, QQ, Video Accounts) | None (free to user)                   | Growth based on social stickiness                  |
| Content / product supply | Yes (mini-program ecosystem)     | Revenue share, technical service fees | Games and mini-program creators pay                |
| Distribution / matching  | Yes (WeChat Ads)                 | Advertising fees                      | Major revenue engine                               |
| Transaction              | No                               | None                                  | Tencent avoids logistics and e-commerce operations |
| Payment                  | Yes (WeChat Pay)                 | Merchant processing fee (~0.6%)       | High-frequency cash cow                            |
| Financial services       | Yes (WeBank, Wealth Management)  | Interest margin, service fees         | High-margin financial business                     |
| Advertising / marketing  | Yes                              | Ads charged by impressions/clicks     | A core profit contributor                          |

**Tencent’s model in one sentence:**
*Monetize social relationships through ads, payments, and service fees.*

---

# 2. Alibaba (E-commerce + Merchant Infrastructure Model)

Alibaba earns primarily from **merchants**, not end users.
It charges at nearly every commercial node.

| Stage                    | Participation       | Revenue Mechanism                              | Notes                                    |
| ------------------------ | ------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| User acquisition         | Yes (Taobao, Tmall) | Free for users, merchants pay for traffic      | Business is B2B2C                        |
| Content / product supply | Yes                 | Annual fees, service fees, store fees          | Tmall charges 30,000–100,000 RMB/year    |
| Distribution / matching  | Yes (Alimama Ads)   | Advertising fees                               | One of Alibaba’s largest revenue streams |
| Transaction              | Yes                 | Commission (0.5%–5%)                           | Marketplace commission model             |
| Fulfillment              | Yes (Cainiao)       | Warehousing, logistics, delivery fees          | Strong supply-chain moat                 |
| Payment                  | Yes (Alipay)        | Payment fee (~0.6%), financial product revenue | Major strategic dataset                  |
| Financial services       | Yes (Ant Group)     | Credit interest spread, insurance, investments | High-margin                              |
| Advertising / marketing  | Yes                 | Large-scale merchant advertising spend         | A necessity for merchants                |

**Alibaba’s model in one sentence:**
*Monetize the entire merchant ecosystem through ads, commissions, and logistics.*

---

# 3. Douyin (Attention + Algorithms + Content E-commerce)

Douyin (TikTok in China) is the strongest **attention → content → commerce** engine.

| Stage                   | Participation           | Revenue Mechanism                           | Notes                                                  |
| ----------------------- | ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| User acquisition        | Yes                     | None                                        | Driven by content and algorithmic distribution         |
| Content supply          | Yes (creator ecosystem) | Live-stream tipping commission (up to ~50%) | Stable revenue stream                                  |
| Product supply          | Yes (Douyin Shops)      | Merchant commissions (1%–10%)               | High-margin categories                                 |
| Distribution / matching | Yes                     | Advertising fees                            | The largest revenue contributor                        |
| Transaction             | Yes                     | E-commerce commission                       | Short-video conversion is highly efficient             |
| Fulfillment             | Limited                 | None                                        | Relies on third-party logistics, gradually integrating |
| Payment                 | Yes (Douyin Pay)        | Low processing fee (~0.38%)                 | Designed to keep users inside a closed loop            |
| Advertising / marketing | Yes (Ocean Engine)      | Advertising spend                           | Top-tier ROI for merchants                             |

**Douyin’s model in one sentence:**
*Monetize attention by converting it directly into advertising and commerce revenue.*

# Summary Table: Business Model Differences

| Stage                   | Tencent              | Alibaba                                | Douyin                        |
| ----------------------- | -------------------- | -------------------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Growth driver           | Social networks      | E-commerce demand                      | Short-form video attention    |
| Supply side             | Mini-programs, UGC   | Merchant products                      | Creator content and merchants |

| Transaction involvement | Low                  | Full marketplace                       | Full marketplace              |

# Three Models in One Line Each

**Tencent:** earns a “relationship tax” through social networks, ads, and payments.
**Alibaba:** earns a “merchant tax” through advertising, commissions, and logistics.
**Douyin:** earns an “attention tax” by converting attention into ads and commerce.

# 腾讯 vs 阿里 vs 抖音：三种商业模型下的各环节收费机制对照表

本文将三家巨头的商业链条拆成六个关键环节：

1. 用户获取
2. 内容或商品供给
3. 分发与匹配
4. 交易与履约
5. 支付与金融
6. 广告与营销

并基于此梳理：
各自参与哪些环节、如何收费、核心盈利点在哪里。

---

# 一、腾讯（社交基础设施 + 流量税 + 支付税模式）

腾讯并不经营电商、物流、供应链，而是提供基础设施，让生态里的商业流动时给腾讯“交税”。

| 环节      | 是否参与         | 收费方式        | 说明         |
| ------- | ------------ | ----------- | ---------- |
| 用户获取    | 是（微信、QQ、视频号） | 不收费         | 社交关系链驱动增长  |
| 内容/商品供给 | 是（小程序生态）     | 分成、技术服务费    | 游戏、小程序均需分成 |
| 分发/匹配   | 是（广告体系）      | 广告费         | 腾讯收入的重要来源  |
| 交易      | 否            | 不收费         | 不做电商闭环     |
| 支付      | 是（微信支付）      | 商户手续费约 0.6% | 高频刚需，现金流稳定 |
| 金融服务    | 是（微粒贷、理财通）   | 利息收入、资金管理费  | 高利润业务      |
| 广告/营销   | 是            | 广告费         | 广告仍是核心营收之一 |

一句话总结：
腾讯通过社交关系和支付体系，向全社会收“基础设施税”。

---

# 二、阿里（电商基础设施 + 商家收费模式）

阿里的商业模式以商家为中心，是典型的“向商家收费”的全链路模式。

| 环节      | 是否参与      | 收费方式              | 说明             |
| ------- | --------- | ----------------- | -------------- |
| 用户获取    | 是（淘宝、天猫）  | 对用户不收费，对商家收流量费    | B 端驱动          |
| 内容/商品供给 | 是         | 年费、店铺费、技术服务费      | 天猫年费 3 万到 10 万 |
| 分发/匹配   | 是（阿里妈妈广告） | 广告费               | 阿里最大收入来源之一     |
| 交易      | 是         | 佣金 0.5% 至 5%      | 类似平台“交易税”      |
| 履约（物流）  | 是（菜鸟）     | 仓储费、配送费、履约费       | 强供应链优势         |
| 支付      | 是（支付宝）    | 支付手续费约 0.6%，理财等收入 | 重要战略入口         |
| 金融      | 是（蚂蚁）     | 信贷利差、保险、消费金融      | 高利润            |
| 广告/营销   | 是         | 广告费               | 商家必须投广告才能获取流量  |

一句话总结：
阿里靠商家体系赚钱，通过广告、佣金、物流形成全链路抽成。

---

# 三、抖音（注意力驱动 + 算法分发 + 内容电商闭环）

抖音是“注意力 → 内容 → 交易”模式的最强实现者。

| 环节    | 是否参与      | 收费方式          | 说明             |
| ----- | --------- | ------------- | -------------- |
| 用户获取  | 是         | 不收费           | 内容驱动自然增长       |
| 内容供给  | 是（创作者体系）  | 直播打赏抽成（约 50%） | 稳定收入           |
| 商品供给  | 是（抖音小店）   | 佣金 1% 至 10%   | 兴趣电商提升转化率      |
| 分发/匹配 | 是         | 广告费           | 巨大的收入来源        |
| 交易    | 是         | 商品佣金          | 在短视频内完成交易      |
| 履约    | 少量（不自建物流） | 无             | 依赖第三方，但逐渐介入供应链 |
| 支付    | 是（抖音支付）   | 低费率约 0.38%    | 为电商闭环服务        |
| 广告/营销 | 是（巨量引擎）   | 广告费           | 目前抖音最重要的营收来源   |

一句话总结：
抖音通过占领注意力，把流量直接转化成广告收入与内容电商佣金。

---

# 四、三家商业模型的核心区别对照

| 环节   | 腾讯       | 阿里         | 抖音        |
| ---- | -------- | ---------- | --------- |
| 增长驱动 | 社交关系链    | 电商需求       | 内容、算法和注意力 |
| 供给侧  | 小程序、内容生态 | 商家商品       | 创作者内容、商家  |
| 分发机制 | 社交流量、广告  | 搜索 + 个性化推荐 | 算法推荐      |
| 交易参与 | 弱        | 强闭环        | 强闭环       |
| 履约能力 | 无        | 菜鸟体系强      | 弱，依赖第三方   |
| 支付   | 微信支付     | 支付宝        | 抖音支付      |
| 主要收入 | 广告、支付    | 商家广告、佣金、物流 | 广告、电商佣金   |

---

# 五、三家公司商业本质的一句话总结

腾讯：赚“关系链带来的流量税”。
阿里：赚“商家的广告和交易税”。
抖音：赚“注意力转化的广告和电商税”。
